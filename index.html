Lokole: email for all

Lokole is Ascoderu's custom device that brings affordable email to unconnected rural communities in Africa.

The capacity to receive, download and share information through electronic networks, the freedom to communicate freely across national boundaries -- these must become realities for all people.

Kofi Annan

Our Mission

Ascoderu aims to connect the unconnected by enabling rural communities to cheaply and sustainably access the global communication email networks and the internet.

We believe that emails can speed up the process of local development by improving education, health services and economic opportunities.

Our mission contributes towards the UN Sustainable Development Goals 9: industry, innovation and infrastructures.

Our History

Ascoderu is a Congolese-Canadian NGO founded by Nzola Swasisa in Victoria BC in 2010.

Between 2010-15, Ascoderu build REC and the team went to the Congo to deploy the proof-of-concept email-device. The responses were positive although the device was unreliable. The proof-of-concept was successful though - and today we got our 2nd prototype called Lokole ready for the next deployments.

Our Team

Nothing would have happen without fourder Nzola Swasisa. Nzola is a native of the African country DRC and while being employed by the humanitarian organization Mission Aviation Fellowship in the Congo, Angola, Namibia, South Africa and Zambia for 19 years, he developed expertise with communications devices and an acute understanding of the communication problems in rural sub-Saharan Africa.

Our Lokole

Lokole is the name of our custom device built on RasberryPi and/or OrangePi. Lokole is a small portable plug-and-play device ready to work wherever there's cellular coverage. Ready for deployments, Lokole devices are ready to change the life of many! Please read more about Lokole.

The name of "Lokole" was proposed by a partner in the Congo as the word refers to the slit drum that traditionally was used for long-distance communication in some areas of the Congo.

Our Lokole Device

How does it work?

  • Plug Lokole with cable or to a battery
  • Lokole produce a 25m(? in) radio WiFi network
  • With your device detect the Lokole wifi and connect
  • Create an account using an user-name and password
  • Send, Receive emails with attachments at the desired daily frequency
