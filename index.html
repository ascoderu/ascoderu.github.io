Lokole: email for all

Lokole is Ascoderu's custom device that brings affordable email to unconnected rural communities in Africa.

The capacity to receive, download and share information through electronic networks, the freedom to communicate freely across national boundaries -- these must become realities for all people.

Kofi Annan

Our Mission

Ascoderu aims to connect the unconnected by enabling rural communities to cheaply and sustainably access the global communication email networks and the internet.

We believe that emails can speed up the process of local development by improving education, health services and economic opportunities.

Our mission contributes towards the UN Sustainable Development Goals 9: industry, innovation and infrastructures.

Our History

Ascoderu is a Congolese-Canadian NGO founded by Nzola Swasisa in Victoria BC in 2010.

Between 2010-15, Ascoderu build REC and the team went to the Congo to deploy the proof-of-concept email-device. The responses were positive although the device was unreliable. The proof-of-concept was successful though - and today we got our 2nd prototype called Lokole ready for the next deployments.

How does Lokole work?

  • Lokole works under cellular coverage
  • To use a Lokole device plug it into a socket or to a battery
  • Lokole creates a permanent 25m/82" radio WiFi network
  • Use any device to connect to the Lokole wifi
  • Go to the Lokole email app URL and create your account
  • The Lokole custom software always enables you to freely:
    • Read already-received emails
    • Download attachments from already-received emails
    • Write and send email (to the Lokole cloud)
  • When the bandwidth is purchased (e.g. once each night*) the Lokole sends the emails stored in the Lokole cloud to the respective recipients.

*Note that the frequency with which a Lokole connects to the internet can be changed, but the less frequent the connetion is the cheapest the service is.
